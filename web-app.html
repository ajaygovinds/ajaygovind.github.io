Introduction

DiShA is a platform designed and developed by Amrita CREATE[1] as a part of Reaching, Impact Saturation and Epidemic Control (RISE), a global project funded by the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID) and implemented by Jhpiego (John Hopkins affiliate). This project was a joint effort between Amrita CREATE, School of Computing Amritapuri, School of Business Amritapuri, and School of Medicine Kochi.

This project addresses the gaps by developing a medical simulation platform with virtual patient cases for critical care, the first in India.



Objective

Develop an engaging gamified simulation platform for emergency healthcare workers to enhance training effectiveness, improve decision-making skills, and boost knowledge retention. The goal is to create a user-friendly interface with adaptable scenarios that encourage teamwork, ensuring healthcare professionals are better prepared for diverse real-life emergencies.

Research & Discovery

On the business logic side, a comprehensive exploration of medical cases was undertaken. We meticulously analyzed a myriad of scenarios, seeking evaluation and validation from expert doctors. This process enabled us to curate extensive case portfolios, ensuring the platform's content was not only diverse but also rigorously assessed for medical accuracy.

Simultaneously, on the platform side, our efforts were directed towards uncovering every conceivable avenue to implement simulations that align with our learning objectives. We delved into various approaches, scrutinizing each for its potential impact on user engagement and educational efficacy. This phase laid the foundation for a robust and versatile simulation framework that caters to the specific needs of emergency healthcare workers.



Ideation & Design Process

Entering the Design phase, we aimed for a fun learning experience. We added an XP system, like in games, where users earn points as they progress through simulations. We made sure the XP aligned with our learning goals, dividing it smartly among simulations.

Starting with wireframes on day one, we moved to mockups after reviews. We kept it nimble‚Äîdesign, client meetings, reviews, and implementation. We rarely built prototypes, saving them for tiny details.

This agile approach helped us swiftly blend gaming vibes into the platform. The result? A user-friendly, engaging tool for emergency healthcare training.



Challenges

Our journey wasn't without its twists. The first curveball came with wireframing. Each medical case had its unique twist, making it tricky to follow a standard design process. But, instead of seeing it as a challenge, we saw it as a lesson‚Äîour platform wasn't a one-size-fits-all template.

Moving on to mockups, the next puzzle was user interaction. With diverse simulations, predicting user moves was like playing chess blindfolded. So, we introduced "game instructions" for each simulation‚Äîa handy guide to keep users in the loop.

Consistency was another tightrope walk. Every simulation had its vibe, but we didn't want users lost in a maze of colors and confusion. Balancing uniqueness and a user-friendly look was our challenge, and we navigated it with care.



Conclusion

Culminating in success, the platform was unveiled with pride at the C20 IHH event at Amrita Hospital, Faridabad. The journey, peppered with challenges, led to a gamified simulation that stands ready to enhance emergency healthcare training. From ideation to execution, this launch marks a new chapter in boosting the skills of frontline professionals.
